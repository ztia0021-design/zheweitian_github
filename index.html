03

Different climate types in Australia have significant effects on annual rainfall. Tropical monsoon regions receive abundant rainfall with distinct wet seasons, while temperate and subtropical regions have moderate and relatively even precipitation. Mediterranean climates experience wet winters and dry summers, whereas inland desert and arid regions receive very little rainfall. In general, coastal and tropical areas tend to have higher annual precipitation.

Australia's climate types strongly influence annual temperature variation. Tropical regions have small temperature ranges, while temperate regions experience moderate seasonal changes. Desert and arid areas show the largest variations due to extreme heat and dryness. Mediterranean climates remain relatively stable with warm summers and mild winters.
